Teriyaki Sauce

Description

This is an easy and delicious teriyaki sauce recipe that comes up in a couple of minutes. You can add this sauce to any cooked protein or veggies for an explosion of flavor. This sauce also keeps well in the fridge in an airtight containter for up to 2 weeks, and keeps well in the freezer for up to 2 months. You can adjust the ingredients as needed. Our constants are equal parts of soy sauce, sake and mirin. This recipe makes around 500ml of sauce or less.

Ingredients

  • 1 cup of Soy Sauce
  • 1 cup of Sake
  • 1 cup of Mirin (Rice Vinegar or Sake works too, just add 1 tbsp of Sugar if subbing)
  • 3 large knobs of garlic, minced
  • 1 large knob of ginger (almost equal to our garlic when minced)
  • 1 tbsp of neutral-tasting oil
  • Sesame seeds to taste
  • 1 cup of chicken stock (optional)
  • 1 tbsp of corn starch and 2 tbsp of water

Steps

  1. Finely mince the garlic and ginger and set to the side.
  2. Add the oil to a hot pot and add the garlic and ginger. Cook for 2 minutes until fragant.
  3. Add the soy sauce, sake and mirin to the pot (the chicken stock if used too). Simmer until the sauce reduces by a third.
  4. Once the sauce has been reduced, prepare a slurry using the corn starch and water. Add to the sauce and stir.
  5. Add the sesame seeds and taste the sauce. Adjust seasoning and sweetness as desired.
  6. Turn off the heat and transfer the sauce to a container or use as desired. Enjoy!
